## Name Meaning and Origin

Luna comes directly from the Latin word *luna*, meaning "moon." In Roman mythology, Luna was the divine personification of the moon --- a goddess who drove a silver chariot across the night sky. She was the counterpart of Sol, the sun god, and her Greek equivalent was Selene.

The moon has been a symbol of femininity, mystery, cycles, and beauty across virtually every culture in human history. By choosing Luna, parents tap into millennia of lunar symbolism --- from the tidal pull of the oceans to the gentle light that guides travelers through darkness.

Luna was rarely used as a given name in English-speaking countries until the 21st century. In Spanish and Italian-speaking cultures, however, Luna had been used as a name for much longer, given that the word is the everyday term for "moon" in both languages. ### Pronunciation Guide

- **English:** LOO-nuh (two syllables, stress on the first)
- **IPA:** /ˈluːnə/
- **Spanish/Italian:** LOO-nah (crisp final syllable)
- **Common mispronunciations:** Occasionally said as "LUH-nuh" in some dialects, but the standard is a long "oo" sound

## Luna in Other Languages and Cultures

Luna is one of those rare names that is the actual word for "moon" in several major languages:

- **Spanish:** Luna (the everyday word for moon)
- **Italian:** Luna (same)
- **Portuguese:** Lua (slightly different)
- **French:** Lune (one letter different)
- **Romanian:** Luna (identical)
- **Latin:** Luna (the original)
- **Japanese:** Can be written as 月 (tsuki, moon) or ルナ (Runa) in katakana

The fact that Luna is a real, recognizable word in so many Romance languages gives it extraordinary international appeal and instant comprehension.

## FAQ

### What does the name Luna mean?

Luna is the Latin word for "moon." In Roman mythology, Luna was the goddess who personified the moon, driving her silver chariot across the night sky. The name carries connotations of beauty, mystery, femininity, and celestial wonder.

### Why has Luna become so popular?

Luna's popularity surge is driven by several factors: the beloved Harry Potter character Luna Lovegood, celebrity parents Chrissy Teigen and John Legend naming their daughter Luna, and the broader cultural trend toward celestial and nature-inspired baby names. Its simplicity and cross-cultural appeal also contribute.

### Is Luna a common name in Spanish-speaking countries?

Yes, Luna has been used as a name in Spanish-speaking countries for longer than in English-speaking ones, since luna is the everyday Spanish word for "moon." It has become increasingly popular in Latin America and Spain in recent years.

### What are good middle names for Luna?

Popular middle names for Luna include Rose, Grace, Mae, Marie, and Celeste. Short, classic middle names provide grounding contrast to Luna's dreamy quality, while celestial options like Celeste create a thematic pairing.
