Henry is the name of kings and the boy next door rolled into one. It manages to sound both aristocratic and down-to-earth, a rare combination that has kept it in the top tier of baby names for generations. With eight English kings, countless literary characters, and a meaning rooted in strength, Henry is a name that commands respect while remaining wonderfully approachable.

> 📌 **Key Takeaway:** Henry is a Germanic name meaning "ruler of the home" from the elements *heim* (home) and *ric* (ruler, power). It currently ranks in the US top 10 for boys and has been climbing steadily since the early 2000s.

## Name Meaning and Origin

Henry derives from the Germanic name *Heimirich*, composed of *heim* ("home, estate") and *ric* ("ruler, power"), giving the meaning "ruler of the home" or "lord of the estate." The name evolved through Old French as Henri before becoming Henry in English.

The name's royal pedigree in England is extraordinary. Eight kings named Henry sat on the English throne, most famously Henry VIII, whose six marriages and break with Rome reshaped English history and religion. Henry V, immortalized by Shakespeare, is remembered as one of England's greatest warrior kings.

Beyond England, the name has been equally prominent across Europe. Henri was borne by four French kings, and Heinrich was common among Holy Roman Emperors and German nobility. This deep royal saturation across multiple countries makes Henry one of the most regal names in Western civilization.

## FAQ

### What does the name Henry mean?

Henry means "ruler of the home" or "lord of the estate." It comes from the Germanic Heimirich, composed of heim (home) and ric (ruler, power). The meaning reflects domestic strength and leadership.

### Is Henry a royal name?

Henry is one of the most royal names in Western history. Eight English kings, four French kings, and numerous Holy Roman Emperors bore the name. Prince Harry's legal name is also Henry. The name carries centuries of aristocratic heritage.

### What is the difference between Henry and Harry?

Harry was the medieval English spoken form of Henry. For centuries, kings named Henry were called Harry in everyday speech. Today, Harry is used both as a nickname for Henry and as an independent name. Prince Harry's legal name, for example, is Henry.

### Is Henry a popular name right now?

Yes, Henry currently ranks in the US top 10 for boy names, having climbed steadily from around rank 100 in the early 2000s. It is also very popular in England, Australia, and across Europe.
