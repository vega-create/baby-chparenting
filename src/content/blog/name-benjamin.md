## Name Meaning and Origin

Benjamin comes from the Hebrew name Binyamin (בִּנְיָמִין), meaning "son of the right hand" or "son of the south." In biblical Hebrew, the "right hand" symbolized strength, favor, and good fortune, making Benjamin a name that connotes being blessed and cherished.

In the Book of Genesis, Benjamin is the youngest of Jacob's twelve sons and the second son of Rachel. His birth was bittersweet --- Rachel died in childbirth, initially naming him Ben-oni ("son of my sorrow"), but Jacob renamed him Benjamin ("son of the right hand"), transforming grief into hope. The Tribe of Benjamin became one of the twelve tribes of Israel.

In American history, Benjamin Franklin and Benjamin Harrison brought the name into the political mainstream. Benjamin Franklin, in particular, gave the name an association with ingenuity, wit, and democratic ideals that endures to this day. ## FAQ

### What does the name Benjamin mean?

Benjamin comes from the Hebrew Binyamin, meaning "son of the right hand" or "son of the south." In biblical Hebrew, the right hand symbolized strength, favor, and blessing, making the name connote a cherished and fortunate child.

### How popular is Benjamin?

Benjamin currently ranks in the US top 10 and has been a top 25 name for decades. It is also popular in France, the UK, and Australia. The name's consistency over more than a century makes it one of the most dependably popular choices available.

### What are the best nicknames for Benjamin?

Benjamin offers outstanding nickname variety: Ben (strong and clean), Benny (warm and classic), Benji (playful and youthful), and Benj (casual and modern). This flexibility is one of the name's greatest strengths.

### Is Benjamin a biblical name?

Yes, Benjamin is a significant biblical name. In the Book of Genesis, Benjamin is the youngest son of Jacob and Rachel and the ancestor of one of the twelve tribes of Israel. The name also appears in the New Testament, as the apostle Paul was from the tribe of Benjamin.
