Choosing a name that means strength is one of the most meaningful gifts you can give your child. A strong name carries weight throughout a lifetime, inspiring confidence and resilience from the very first day. Parents across cultures have long selected names rooted in power and fortitude, hoping to set an intention for the kind of person their child will become. Whether you draw inspiration from ancient warriors, natural forces, or timeless virtues, a name that embodies strength never goes out of style.

From the Germanic roots of names like Matilda to the Hebrew origins of Ethan, strength-inspired names span every language and tradition. Many of these names have surged in popularity in recent years as parents seek options that feel both classic and empowering. Below you will find a curated list of 50 names organized by gender, each with its origin and full meaning to help you find the perfect fit.

> 📌 **Key Takeaway:** Names meaning strength come from dozens of languages and cultures. Whether you prefer a bold, recognizable classic like Andrew or a rare gem like Valentina, there is a powerful name waiting for your baby. ## How to Choose a Name That Means Strength

Selecting a name is deeply personal. Consider how the name sounds with your surname, whether it honors a cultural tradition, and how it might grow with your child from infancy to adulthood. Names meaning strength pair especially well with softer middle names for balance, or you can double down with two powerful names for maximum impact.

## FAQ

### What is the most popular baby name meaning strength?

Ethan and Liam consistently rank among the most popular boy names meaning strength in the United States. For girls, Matilda and Valentina have risen sharply in recent years. These names combine strong etymological roots with mainstream appeal, making them safe yet meaningful choices.

### Are there gender-neutral names that mean strength?

Yes, several unisex names carry meanings of strength. Emery, meaning brave and powerful from German origins, works beautifully for any gender. Other options include Phoenix, Casey, and Riley, all of which have strength or courage woven into their meanings.

### Can I find names meaning strength from non-European origins?

Absolutely. Kenzo comes from Japanese and means strong and healthy. Kali has Sanskrit roots meaning fierce strength. Takeshi is another Japanese name meaning fierce warrior. Many African, Arabic, and Asian languages also offer beautiful names rooted in power and resilience.
