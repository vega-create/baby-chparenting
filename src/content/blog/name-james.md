James may be the most reliably popular name in the English language. It has never once fallen out of the US top 20 since records began in 1880 --- a feat no other name can claim. Presidential, biblical, and effortlessly cool, James is the name that simply never goes out of style. Whether you are considering it for your firstborn or your fourth, here is your complete guide to this indestructible classic.

> 📌 **Key Takeaway:** James is a Hebrew-origin name meaning "supplanter," derived from the biblical Jacob. It has ranked in the US top 20 for over 140 consecutive years --- an unmatched record of consistency.

## Name Meaning and Origin

James derives from the Late Latin name Iacomus, which evolved from the Hebrew name Ya'aqov (Jacob), meaning "supplanter" or "one who follows." The name traveled through Greek (Iakobos), into Late Latin (Iacomus), and finally into Old French (James) before arriving in English.

In the New Testament, James is the name of two of Christ's apostles --- James the Greater and James the Less. This apostolic connection made the name extremely important in Christian cultures. In the medieval period, the pilgrimage route to Santiago de Compostela (Saint James's shrine in Spain) spread the name's influence throughout Europe.

In England and Scotland, the name James has a deeply royal history. Six Scottish kings bore the name, and when James VI of Scotland became James I of England in 1603, uniting the two crowns, the name became inseparable from British royalty and power.

## FAQ

### What does the name James mean?

James means "supplanter" or "one who follows." It derives from the Hebrew name Ya'aqov (Jacob) through Late Latin Iacomus and Old French. The meaning comes from the biblical story of Jacob grasping his twin brother Esau's heel at birth.

### Has James always been a popular name?

Yes, James has been in the US top 20 continuously since government records began in 1880 --- over 140 years without interruption. It held the number 1 spot for decades in the early-to-mid 20th century and remains in the top 5 today.

### Is James a good middle name?

James is one of the most popular middle names in the English-speaking world. Its single syllable, strong consonant ending, and universal familiarity make it flow well after almost any first name. It is an excellent choice for parents who want a classic, dignified middle name.

### What is James in other languages?

James has many international forms including Diego and Jaime in Spanish, Jacques in French, Giacomo in Italian, Seamus in Irish, and Hamish in Scottish Gaelic. Santiago, the Spanish for Saint James, is also part of the James name family.
