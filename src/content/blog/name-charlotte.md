## Name Meaning and Origin

Charlotte is the French feminine diminutive of Charles, which derives from the Germanic name Karl, meaning "free man." By extension, Charlotte carries the meaning "free woman" or, given its diminutive form, "petite and feminine." The name entered English usage through French influence, particularly after the Norman Conquest.

Charlotte first became fashionable in royal circles. Queen Charlotte of Mecklenburg-Strelitz (1744--1818), wife of King George III, brought the name to prominence in Britain. The city of Charlotte, North Carolina, was named in her honor. The name continued its aristocratic tradition into the 21st century when Prince William and Catherine Middleton named their daughter Princess Charlotte in 2015.

The literary pedigree is equally impressive. Charlotte Bronte, one of the great Victorian novelists, and the character Charlotte in E.B. White's *Charlotte's Web* have both contributed to the name's beloved status in English-speaking culture. ## Famous People Named Charlotte

The name Charlotte has distinguished bearers across literature, royalty, and entertainment:

1. **Charlotte Bronte** --- English novelist and poet, author of the groundbreaking *Jane Eyre* and one of the famous Bronte sisters.
2. **Princess Charlotte of Cambridge** --- Daughter of Prince William and Catherine, Princess of Wales, born in 2015.
3. **Charlotte Gainsbourg** --- Franco-British actress and singer known for her work in European and American cinema.
4. **Charlotte Perkins Gilman** --- American writer and social reformer, author of the influential short story *The Yellow Wallpaper*.
5. **Charlotte Rampling** --- Acclaimed English actress known for decades of work in both art-house and mainstream films.

## FAQ

### What does the name Charlotte mean?

Charlotte is the French feminine diminutive of Charles, which comes from the Germanic Karl, meaning "free man." Charlotte therefore means "free woman" or "petite." The name carries connotations of independence, femininity, and strength.

### Is Charlotte a royal name?

Yes, Charlotte has a strong royal heritage. Queen Charlotte of Mecklenburg-Strelitz was the wife of King George III of England. Most recently, Princess Charlotte of Cambridge, born in 2015, brought the name back into the royal spotlight. Multiple European royal houses have used the name over the centuries.

### What are the best nicknames for Charlotte?

Charlotte offers excellent nickname options including Charlie (spunky and gender-neutral), Lottie (British and adorable), Char (short and casual), and Lotte (European flair). This versatility is one of Charlotte's greatest strengths as a given name.

### How popular is Charlotte right now?

Charlotte currently ranks in the top 5 girl names in the United States. It has been in the top 10 since the mid-2010s and shows no signs of declining. The name also ranks highly in the UK, Australia, and Canada.
